How does climate change affect fungal growth and activity?
How do fungi mediate ecosystem responses to climatic variations?

Fungi play a dominant role in the natural kingdom. Therefore, modifications in fungal growth resulting from global climate shifts will have significant effects on the ecosystem functions. Meteorological changes are already impacting mushrooms life, but numerous are still the questions and knowledge gaps about the behaviour of this complex system.
Matsutake (Tricholoma matsutake) is a wild mushroom known as the marker of the autumn season*. It is a mycorrhizal fungus that, thanks to a symbiotic relationship with trees, helps the forests grow in areas destroyed by human occupancy.
Appeared firstly in Japan, the history of matsutake talks about travellers, economy and today, more than ever, environmental emergency. As a precious example of collaboration between different species, the impact of the climate crisis and unethical human actions over this fungus is an urgent concern.

The fruiting of matsutake has been studied in the village of Haitangwa, located in the east of Yunnan Province around Baoshan City, China. Scientists identified, in this region, irregularities in the season of the fungi productivity as a consequence of climate change. Three are the primary variables affecting matsutake growth: temperature, humidity and precipitation. Nevertheless, the lack of long term datasets makes not yet possible comprehend the mushrooms evolution in regional climate patterns.
Is it possible to apply new methods to interpret the climate-fruiting relationship?
Autumn Season is a web application that visualises and archive climate data. The project is divided into three main phases:
. 1 collection
. 2 analysis
. 3 simulations

On July 1, 2020, phase 1 started, saving every 24 hours, weather data over the selected area. The natural course of time guides this phase. It is necessary to wait for, at least, one year to have the right amount of data over the four seasons. After one year, the project will go through the second phase, the analysis and visualisation of the acquired data. Phase 3 will use emerging technologies to predict and speculate about the fungi evolution in responses to current and future environmental changes.
